About

I am a PhD candidate in Polymer Chemistry at the University of Connecticut, working in the Material Science Lab under the Adamson Research Group. My research focuses on the synthesis, nano-architecture, and application of advanced materials including graphene-based composites, conductive polymers, and functional nanomaterials.

Beyond the lab, I am an NSF-UCONN Entrepreneurship Fellow and founder of AeroGraphiX, a startup developing revolutionary air filtration technology. I bring the same passion to teaching—having taught chemistry at multiple institutions, I believe in hands-on learning and making complex concepts accessible to diverse learners.

My journey spans continents and disciplines: from Banaras Hindu University in India where I earned my MS in Chemistry, to teaching roles across Connecticut, to cutting-edge materials research at UConn. I thrive at the intersection of research, innovation, and education.

Research

My research interests center on synthesis, nano-architecture, and applications of conductive polymers, composites, adhesives, and paints.

Graphene & h-BN Composites

Developing graphene and hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) based composites using the Surface Interface Trapping Method (SITM). These composites leverage synergistic properties—h-BN incorporation mitigates graphene's zero bandgap, enabling tunable electronic properties with superior thermal management for electronic and optoelectronic devices.

Functional Nanostructured Materials

Creating functional nanostructured materials comprising graphene oxide and butyl acrylate using ATRP and Click chemistry. This work includes synthesis, molecular design, functionalization, and comprehensive characterization of novel materials.

Students learn best by doing—through lab work, experiments, and active engagement with materials. Complex concepts should be broken down for diverse learners, whether nursing students encountering chemistry for the first time or advanced undergraduates. And every student deserves individualized attention and support to succeed.
